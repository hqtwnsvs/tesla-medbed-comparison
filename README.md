## The Five-Layer Tech, Broken Down Plainly

Most mats in this price tier lean on one or two technologies. Tesla MedBed X stacks five into the same surface. Here's what each layer actually does, in plain language.

*Far-infrared heat.* Not the surface warmth of a heating pad. Far-infrared waves penetrate a few centimeters into tissue, which is why people describe the sensation as "deep" rather than "hot on the skin." Adjustable temperature lets you dial it from gentle toasty to noticeably warm.

*PEMF.* Pulsed electromagnetic fields. The mat sends low-intensity magnetic pulses through coils embedded in the surface. PEMF is the modality people usually buy this category of device for — it's the same technology used in clinical recovery settings, just at lower intensity for home use.

*Red-light LEDs.* Photonic wavelengths that complement the heat and PEMF. Not a replacement for a dedicated red-light panel, but a useful add-on when layered with the other modalities.

*Negative ions.* The same charged particles you breathe near waterfalls or after a thunderstorm. Built-in emitters try to recreate that "fresh air" feeling indoors.

*Gemstones.* Amethyst, tourmaline, obsidian, jade, and bio-ceramic elements are layered through the mat. Beyond the metaphysical associations, the practical job of the stones is to conduct infrared heat evenly and emit far-infrared naturally when warmed.

**Tesla MedBed X Review Summary:** Five modalities, one mat, no medical claims. The pitch is consolidation — replace a stack of single-purpose devices with one surface that does all of them at once.

## Daily Use: From Box to Bedtime

Setup is genuinely simple. Here's the routine most users settle into.

1. **Unroll the mat on a flat surface.** Bed, couch, massage table, or floor all work. The flexible design means you're not locked into one spot.

2. **Connect the controller and plug into a standard outlet.** No special wiring, no installation appointment.

3. **Start low for your first session.** 15–20 minutes at gentle heat and low PEMF. Your body needs to acclimate before you crank things up.

4. **Build up over the first two to four weeks.** Increase duration to 20–40 minutes. Try morning sessions for energy or evening sessions for sleep — see which timing your body prefers.

5. **Settle into a consistent routine by month two.** Some users do short daily sessions; others prefer longer sessions three or four times a week. Consistency matters more than intensity.

6. **Hydrate before and after.** Infrared heat and PEMF both pull water through your system. Skip this and you'll feel it.

7. **Let the mat cool before storing.** Roll it gently — sharp folds damage the internal coils and gemstone layers.
